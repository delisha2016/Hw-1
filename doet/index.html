Usability Problems in Everyday Objects

Problem 1: Washing Machine Control Panel

What is wrong with it?

The control panel on the washing machine dial is difficult to read. The symbols are not intuitive, especially for first-time users, and the layout is cluttered with too many options.

Usability Issues:

  • Learnability: New users may struggle to operate the machine without consulting the manual.
  • Efficiency: Small, cramped buttons make laundry tasks more time-consuming.
  • Safety: Misreading the controls could lead to incorrect settings, like using the wrong water temperature.

Suggested Fixes:

  • Add text labels next to symbols for clarity.
  • Use color coding to differentiate cycles quickly.
  • Increase icon size for better visibility.

Problem 2: Inserting Batteries

Description:

Inserting batteries can be confusing due to unclear markings, and they are often difficult to remove. Additionally, users have no way to know when the batteries are close to dying.

Usability Issues:

  • Learnability: Difficulty in identifying the correct battery orientation.
  • Efficiency: Lack of battery life indicators leads to unexpected device shutdowns.
  • Safety: Misuse could result in battery leakage, overheating, or explosion.

Suggested Fixes:

  • Add battery life indicators (e.g., LED lights).
  • Use a textured surface for better grip during removal.
  • Color-code polarity markings for easier identification.

Problem 3: Elevator Buttons

What is wrong with it?

The elevator buttons are poorly labeled, clustered together, and difficult to use, especially in emergencies.

Usability Issues:

  • Learnability: Lack of clear labels makes it harder to find the correct button quickly.
  • Efficiency: A cluttered layout can cause delays as users search for the right button.
  • Safety: The help button is placed too low, making it inaccessible in an emergency.

Suggested Fixes:

  • Organize buttons logically and label them clearly.
  • Reposition the help button at an accessible height.
  • Group related buttons (e.g., floor numbers together, function buttons in another area).

Conclusion

Usability issues in everyday objects can significantly impact user experience. By focusing on key dimensions such as Learnability, Efficiency, and Safety, designers can create more intuitive and user-friendly products.
